# Hadex Exploration

## Shared Message Ladder

Every concept should communicate this sequence in roughly this order:

1. Dex is a personal operating system, not another AI chat box.
2. It helps people become visibly better at work.
3. It is free forever for individuals and open source.
4. It is local-first: your data stays with you and works with both cloud models and downloaded open models.
5. Diffs let people in similar roles see how AI changes real work.
6. That visible leverage is what helps peers and teams buy in.
7. Dex Pi is the premium harness for people who want the deepest orchestration and intelligence.

## Proof Stack

Every direction should pull from the same proof ingredients, even if the order changes:

- GitHub CTA and star signal
- Founder credibility
- Build-in-public energy
- LinkedIn article / announcement proof
- Podcast proof
- Product glimpses that show real work, not fake dashboards
- Diff examples that make role-to-role learning obvious

## What Must Stay True

- Free forever for individuals
- Open source is a trust signal, not a side note
- Local-first matters
- Dex is for non-technical professionals, not just builders
- Dex Pi is the premium step-up, not the whole story
- Diff sharing is the bridge from individual adoption to team expansion

## What To Avoid

- Generic AI SaaS gradients
- Empty productivity language
- Fake enterprise polish with no soul
- Overly technical copy
- Framing Dex as just another assistant
- Hiding the free/open-source offer behind premium messaging

## Visual Territories

### 1. Editorial Manifesto

- High-contrast black and pink
- Sharp type hierarchy
- Movement energy
- Founder-led point of view

### 2. Operating System Interface

- Product surface first
- Live context panels
- Command-center feel
- Morning brief and workflow transformation visuals

### 3. Open Source Credibility

- Repository, stars, install path, trust
- Calm confidence over hype
- Premium Pi appears later in the journey

### 4. Role-To-Role Diff Network

- Peer learning as the hero
- Role clusters and workflow cards
- Social proof built from transformations, not testimonials alone

### 5. Premium Harness Reveal

- Free Dex as the entry point
- Dex Pi as the advanced layer
- Registration wall used as intrigue and gravity

### 6. Human Chief Of Staff

- Softer editorial tone
- Emotional relief and cognitive load reduction
- Still premium, but less technical-looking

## Screen Requirements

Each concept should include:

- Homepage hero
- Two supporting sections near the top
- Mid-page proof section
- Dex Pi conversion moment
- Diff landing treatment
- Public diff profile treatment

## Shortlist Criteria

We will score the concepts against:

- Clarity of the free-to-premium ladder
- Strength of the diff story
- Memorability
- Distance from commodity AI design
- Ease of implementation in this repo

## Current Shortlist

### 1. Open Source Credibility

Why it wins:

- Best at making the free/open-source/local-first story instantly believable
- Cleanest ladder from trust to Dex Pi
- Strong contrast from generic AI sites because it feels calm and earned
- Likely easiest to implement well in the current website stack

### 2. Role-To-Role Diff Network

Why it wins:

- Best expression of the diff thesis
- Makes team spread feel natural rather than forced
- Gives Hadex its own category language instead of borrowing generic AI tropes
- Strong strategic bridge from individual adoption to company pull

### 3. Operating System Interface

Why it wins:

- Best at showing that Dex is more than a chat product
- Most product-legible without becoming dashboard sludge
- Makes Dex Pi feel like a deeper operating layer instead of a random upsell
- Strong candidate for a future product-led landing variant

## Deprioritised Directions

### Editorial Manifesto

Strong and memorable, but a little too close to founder manifesto mode for the main default landing.

### Premium Harness Reveal

Good energy, but too premium-forward for the default entry experience.

### Human Chief Of Staff

Warm and approachable, but weaker at expressing the diff/community wedge.
